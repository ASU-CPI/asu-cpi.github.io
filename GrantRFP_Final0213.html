Innovative Broadband Use Evaluation and Research Data Seed Grant

Request for Proposals

BCC: Broadband Use Mapping, Data and Evaluation. National Science Foundation. Building Community and Capacity for Data-Intensive Research in the Social, Behavioral, and Economic Sciences and in Education and Human Resources (BCC-SBE/EHR). NSF Proposal# 1338471

Organization Background:

In partnership with the University of Iowa, the Arizona State University School of Public Affairs (SPA) and Center for Policy Informatics (CPI) are soliciting proposals for a project for the collection of new forms of data on broadband and Internet use, funded through NSF 1338471.  The data funded through this RFP will be shared through a repository and will promote research on the potential benefits or consequences of broadband use.

Project Description:

This RFP provides the basis for the  award of three $10,000 seed grants for projects that generate innovative data on broadband or mobile Internet use and new methods for data collection.  This seed grant award is part of a larger National Science Foundation-funded project that will make broadband and Internet use data, program evaluations and reports available through a web-based data portal being constructed to serve as a repository for this data and for the wider broadband research community.  Researchers who are awarded support through this RFP must agree to make the data available through the repository.

Proposals may address broadband and Internet use by organizations or individuals.  An ever increasing amount of information has migrated online, which could be used for research on democratic participation, political engagement, government services, health care, economic opportunities, energy use, transportation use, and environmental quality among many other topics.   A large portion of existing research on the social impacts of the Internet to date has been related to the availability of new broadband infrastructure. In order to investigate the impacts and disparities related to information technology, researchers need better data on the myriad ways in which digital technologies are being used by individuals and organizations – on the activities that are evolving from high-speed Internet use.

Design Requirements:

Funded projects must be designed to generate original data on broadband or Internet use, and can include pilots to explore the feasibility of larger-scale efforts later. Eligible proposals will collect data from or about broadband use, and could include data from mobile devices or sensors, social media, websites, public opinion surveys, other surveys, records, government documents, GIS data or other sources.  Proposals will need to explain how the data, methodology, or methods of collection are innovative, and how this will make a contribution to research on Internet use.   Funded projects must provide resulting data as a deliverable and be posted on the web-based data portal to support the wider broadband research community.  Researchers will also be required to present their project at a conference that will be held in Phoenix at Arizona State University in February 2015.  Expenses for travel and conference attendance will be covered for participants, in addition to the maximum $10,000 funding for the seed grant.

Proposal Requirements:

Proposal submissions will be a maximum of 10 single-spaced pages or less, including budget and references.  The seed fund proposal should discuss the contribution and significance of the proposed data, why it is innovative, how it will be collected, methods of analysis or ways in which the data can be analyzed or used by the researcher and others, a description of the data to be delivered, and qualifications of the researcher(s).  A brief budget and budget justification must be included.

 In addition, a two-page curriculum vitae must be submitted with highlights from five related publications, as well five other selected publications, and selected grant projects. 

Project Duration:

Funded applicants will be notified by April 15, 2014, with funds made available at that time.   Final data and analysis deliverables will be due by February 28, 2015.

Agreements:

Awardees agree to deliver preliminary data results at a research conference being hosted at ASU in early February  2015.  Final data deliverables must be submitted by February 28, 2015 at the end of the project period.
